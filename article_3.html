Astronaut Don Pettit shows how to take long-exposure photos from the ISS

Science

Mark Wilson

17 Dec 2024

Astronaut Don Pettit has become famous for his long-exposure photographs taken aboard the International Space Station (ISS). Using a DSLR camera and custom setups, Pettit captured stunning images of Earth, showcasing star trails, city lights, auroras, and lightning storms. These photographs, taken with exposures lasting 10-15 minutes, provide a unique perspective of our planet and the movement of the ISS as it orbits at high speeds.

To achieve these shots, Pettit had to overcome the challenges of microgravity and the rapid motion of the ISS. He used specially adapted equipment, such as a modified tripod for use in zero gravity, and took careful consideration of the camera’s settings to create these breathtaking images.


Pettit’s photography blends science and art, offering a new way to appreciate the beauty of our world from space. His work has inspired many, showing how technology and creativity can combine to reveal stunning views of Earth and the universe beyond.
